What is Braillie?

Braille is a system of raised dots that can be felt with the fingertips and is used by people who are blind or visually impaired to read and write

Let's Know More About Braille

Wanna know a fun fact about braille? Braille inititially started out as a military code in the army

Louis Braille is the inventor of the braille code. He was born on January 4, 1809, in Coupvray, France. At the age of 3, while playing in his father's shop, Louis injured his eye on a sharp tool. Despite the best care available at the time, infection set in and soon spread to the other eye, leaving him completely blind.

In his mid 20's Louis Braille got his inspiration to use embossed dots to represent letters after he watched Charles Barbier demonstrate a note-taking system he invented of embossed dots to represent sounds The army was not impressed, so Barbier brought his system to the school for the blind. Louis immediately recognized its merits and spent the next three years improving upon Barbier's idea.

Hence, this led to the invention or founding of the braille script for the blind by Louis Braille in the year 1824. This invention is now celebrated each year on 4th January which marks the birth of Louis Braille.
